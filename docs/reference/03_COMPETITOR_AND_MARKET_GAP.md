# Competitor and Market Gap

## Referenced Competitors

### Climbro Mini

Strengths:

- portable
- app-driven
- approachable user experience

Weaknesses relative to GripSense:

- total-force focus
- no per-finger distribution
- no finger-specific compensation analysis

### Tindeq Progressor

Strengths:

- known in the climbing market
- portable
- useful for peak force and RFD

Weaknesses relative to GripSense:

- total force only
- limited insight into how individual fingers share the load

### Force Board Portable

Strengths:

- strong no-hang format
- useful for force-time metrics
- useful hand-to-hand comparison

Weaknesses relative to GripSense:

- not per finger
- limited visibility into internal hand load sharing

## Real Gap

The gap is not just "another force device."

The gap is a product that can show:

- what each finger contributes
- which fingers compensate
- which fingers fail first
- how force redistributes over time

## Strategic Implication

GripSense should not compete only on generic strength metrics.

Its strongest position is:

- per-finger insight
- deeper interpretation
- asymmetry and compensation visibility
- relevance for both performance and rehab-adjacent use

## Positioning Line

Others measure how strong the hand is overall.

GripSense measures what each finger actually does.
